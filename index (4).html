THE STUDIO

I have been drawing since I was a child. Not because anyone told me to — it just felt like the most natural thing. When it was time to choose a path, I thought fashion made sense. Eleven years later, across studios and international markets, I realised something quietly uncomfortable: none of it was mine. Fashion is for someone else. You're always dressing another body, another season, another brief. Art is the first thing I've done entirely for myself — and somehow, it's the thing that makes other people feel seen.

"I don't plan a painting. I pick a colour that feels right that day, and I follow it. By the end, it has become something I didn't know I needed to make."

THE PRACTICE

The work spans paintings, jewellery, sculpture and prints — but medium has never been the point. Feeling is the point. Everything I make comes from the same place: an internal state I don't always have words for. I am self-taught. I learned by doing, by failing, by starting over with a different colour. I overthink everything in life — but when I'm working, the overthinking quiets. The instinct takes over. That's the only place I fully trust myself.

I believe in energy — not ritual, not rules. The work carries whatever was in me when I made it. A painting made in grief looks different from one made in joy, even if the subject is the same. People feel it. They don't always know why — but they feel it.

ON PLACE

I grew up in Alwar, Rajasthan. After a decade in NCR for work, I now split my time between the two — half the year in the city, half at home with my mother. The colours of Alwar — the dust, the sky after monsoon, the gold in the afternoon light — I don't paint them consciously, but they're in everything I make.

If something I've made makes you feel like you've been seen — like it was made for exactly where you are right now — then it worked.

The practice

Paintings

Archival giclée prints. The image distilled to its essence — made to last.

Handcrafted accessories at the intersection of fashion and fine art. Each piece carries eleven years of atelier intuition — made to be felt as much as seen.
